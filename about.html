Who am I?

While the main focus here is of course my professional life, it doesn't hurt to add some information about what I'm like outside of work. If this isn't something you're interested in, please don't keep reading! Or do, it doesn't really matter to me. In any case, I spend most of my free time on my PC, reading books, and baking. A special mention goes to my dearest dog Kobe, who keeps me busy and happy.

The Life of a Tech Nerd

I've been a tech nerd since I was in elementary school, and I love keeping up with new technologies and finding out new ways to use tech. This portfolio is perhaps a good indicator of these things; I don't really need to do this, but alas, here I am. A lot of the time I spend on my PC I spend playing video games. I wrote my bachelor's thesis on one of my favorite video games of all time, I wrote articles about gaming in my university's online magazine, I could keep listing my gaming-related merits for a while. All that just to express my love for video games. Let's move on.

Dear Reader

When I moved to my current apartment six months ago, I had to buy a new bookshelf. This bookshelf is now too small, and I need to buy a new one, again. I only buy books that I genuinely love. I think it's safe to say that I really love books! Doesn't matter whether it's literary fiction, self-help or romance; I will want to read it. I could spend hours in a bookstore looking at books and reading the back covers. I have spent hours reading the back covers.

'Cause the baker's gonna bake

One of the greatest joys in my life is to host dinner parties, and I always bake a slab of focaccia to serve. It doesn't matter what the theme is, focaccia it is. I went through a sourdough phase a year or so ago, which ultimately ended in my "undying" sourdough starter dying. Seriously, I was told that it was near impossible to kill a starter, but I killed mine. After that I have stuck to instant yeast and other leaveners with great results. Is my freezer full of baked goods? Yes. Is that a bad thing? Maybe?
